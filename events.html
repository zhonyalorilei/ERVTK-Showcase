Cyber Robo Challenge



The Cyber Robo Challenge (Cybersecurity in Robotics & Automation) competition in cybersecurity is a competitive event where participants, attempt to solve a series of security-related challenges to "capture flags" or specific pieces of information hidden within a simulated or real-world environment. These challenges typically involve various aspects of cybersecurity, such as cryptography, reverse engineering, web exploitation, network analysis, and more. Participants gain points or rankings by successfully solving these challenges, demonstrating their skills in identifying vulnerabilities and protecting systems from cyber threats. This competition serve as valuable training grounds for honing cybersecurity skills and fostering a deeper understanding of security concepts.

Freestyle



Any robot or programmed electronic / mechanical project can compete. The robot can have any function. This is the category for innovators, inventors, and entrepreneurs to showcase their creation. The team that developed the robot or electronic projected must present it in a Freestyle Exhibition which will extend one or two days of competition and then to a jury. Line Following Classic



Line follower Classic is an autonomous robot which follows either a black line on a white surface or a white line on a black surface. The robot must be able to detect the particular line and keep following it. The line is simple, it does not have interruptions, sharp corners or bifurcations. There is not age limit for Line Follower Classic.

Line Following ZMROBO and UBTECH



The fundamental principle of the line follower competition involving ZMrobo and UBTech robots is that participants are only allowed to use official ZMROBO and UBtech parts, and they must adhere to standard construction methods without the use of any additional means such as tape, glue, stickers, etc. This category is designed for young robotics enthusiasts aiming to compete at a beginner level alongside other participants.

Mega Sumo



The Mega Sumo category is the most spectacular category of robots, two robots are placed on a 1.5m diameter steel ring, where they fight autonomously against each other until one is removed from the ring. Each robot of mega sumo must be within the 3 kg weight limit and in the size of 200 x 200mm. The robot must be fully autonomous and be able to start and stop on command from the referee via star/stop module (see appendix in the regulations).

Mini Sumo



Similar to Mega Sumo, Mini Sumo robots must be within the 0.5kg weight limit and in the size limit of 100 x 100mm. The battle ring has a diameter or 800mm made of non magnetic material. The robot must be fully autonomous and be able to start and stop on command from the referee via star/stop module (see appendix in the regulations).

Robosim Competition



In a ROBOSIM competition, participants often design and program virtual robots, which are then tested within the simulated environment. This allows participants to experiment, refine their algorithms, and test their robots' capabilities without the need for physical hardware.

Virtual Robot Competition



Virtual Robot Simulator (VRS) is a computer-based software or system that emulates the behavior and environment of physical robots, allowing users to design, test, and simulate robotic operations without the need for actual hardware. It serves as a virtual platform for developing and experimenting with robotics applications, enabling engineers and researchers to refine algorithms and control strategies before deploying them to real-world robots.
